About Me

Welcome to my academic profile. I am a passionate researcher in Computational Materials Science with a strong interest in nanomaterials and machine learning applications. I recently completed my Master's degree at Mazandaran University. I am actively seeking PhD opportunities to advance my research in predicting the mechanical properties of advanced materials using atomic simulations and deep learning.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Materials Science, where I focus on developing innovative approaches to modeling and predicting the mechanical behavior of nanomaterials like graphene and carbon nanotubes. I am particularly drawn to molecular dynamics simulations and deep learning models and their applications in understanding fracture properties and atomic-scale phenomena.

I am committed to conducting impactful research that addresses real-world challenges and contributes to the advancement of knowledge in my field. I am eager to collaborate with leading researchers and contribute to groundbreaking discoveries.

Research Interests

Primary Research Area

My primary research area is the investigation of the mechanical properties of nanomaterials, specifically graphene and carbon nanotubes, using atomic-scale modeling and deep learning. This field is significant because nanomaterials possess unique mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties that make them critical for applications in nanotechnology, energy storage, and advanced composites. Understanding their behavior at the atomic level is essential for designing materials with tailored properties for real-world applications. I am particularly compelled by the challenge of predicting complex mechanical behaviors, such as fracture and deformation, under varying conditions. My preliminary work includes a molecular dynamics study on the mechanical and fracture properties of carbon polycrystalline graphene and nanotubes, published as a first-author article titled Molecular Dynamics Studies on the Mechanical and Fracture Properties of Carbon Polycrystalline Graphene and Nanotube. This work has laid the foundation for developing predictive models that combine atomic simulations with machine learning to enhance material design efficiency.

Secondary Research Focus

My secondary research focus is the application of artificial intelligence (AI), particularly deep learning, to predict material properties and optimize material discovery in computational materials science. This area connects to my primary research by providing a data-driven approach to complement and accelerate atomic modeling. By training deep learning models on simulation data, I aim to predict mechanical properties with high accuracy, reducing the computational cost of traditional methods like molecular dynamics. My unique perspective lies in integrating domain-specific knowledge from materials science with advanced AI techniques, enabling the development of robust predictive models. This synergy allows me to explore complex material behaviors that are difficult to capture through simulations alone, such as defect propagation or multi-scale interactions in nanomaterials.

Interdisciplinary Connections

My research interests span computational materials science, artificial intelligence, and computational physics, fostering opportunities for innovative cross-disciplinary work. By combining molecular dynamics simulations with deep learning, I aim to bridge the gap between physics-based modeling and data-driven predictions. This interdisciplinary approach enables the exploration of material properties at multiple scales, from atomic to macroscopic levels. I am particularly interested in leveraging machine learning techniques, such as graph neural networks, to model atomic interactions and predict material behavior under diverse conditions. Additionally, my interest in computational biology allows me to explore parallels between material systems and biological structures, such as protein folding or biomaterial design, potentially leading to advancements in bioinspired materials. This cross-disciplinary perspective enhances the potential for discovering novel materials with applications in both engineering and biomedical fields.

Future Directions

My long-term research vision is to develop a comprehensive framework for predicting and designing the mechanical properties of nanomaterials using a hybrid approach that integrates atomic-scale simulations with advanced machine learning models. In the near term, I plan to expand my work on graphene and carbon nanotubes by investigating their behavior under extreme conditions, such as high temperatures or radiation, using multi-scale modeling. I also aim to develop generalizable deep learning models that can predict the properties of a wide range of nanomaterials, accelerating material discovery for applications in energy, aerospace, and biomedicine. My ultimate goal is to contribute to the development of sustainable, high-performance materials that address global challenges, such as energy efficiency and environmental resilience. By fostering collaborations across materials science, AI, and computational biology, I hope to make impactful contributions to the advancement of next-generation materials and technologies.
